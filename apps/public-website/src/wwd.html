Self-advocacy

Self-advocacy is a vital skill, empowering participants to express their needs, make informed choices, and take greater control over their lives. When individuals can effectively communicate their thoughts, preferences, and concerns, they become active participants in their own care, relationships, and daily experiences. At ADE, we believe that fostering self-advocacy not only builds confidence and independence—it also promotes dignity, respect, and inclusion in all areas of life. Key areas of focus include choice-making about activities and within activities, assertiveness and appropriate ways to refuse or state wants and needs, appropriate relationship interaction, building and maintaining friendships, opportunities for day-to-day planning activities, making informed choices through discussion of both positive and negative consequences, and personal awareness and safety, along with other assistance in increasing control over the individual’s own life and environment.

Domestic and Self Care

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur.

Developing domestic and self-care skills is essential for fostering independence. These skills enable individuals to participate more fully in their home and community environments. At ADE, we incorporate these life skills into our daily programming to promote autonomy, build confidence, and support each person in achieving greater self-sufficiency. Focus areas include self-care and personal hygiene, nutrition, meal planning and preparation using easy and safe methods such as a microwave, toaster oven, countertop grill, and crock-pot, money management and budgeting, and basic housekeeping.

Vocational Training

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur.

At ADE, individuals are encouraged to express their unique interests and preferences when it comes to vocational pursuits. We offer pre-vocational training and support designed to help participants build essential job skills and positive work habits that may lead to future paid employment. Our focus areas include listening and attention skills, following multi-step directions, and workplace readiness and responsibility. These foundational skills are key to success in integrated work environments. When individuals are ready to take the next step, ADE coordinates with Alta California Regional Center (ACRC) to explore employment opportunities through their supported work programs.

Engaging in enjoyable, purposeful activities helps reduce stress, build social connections, improve physical health, and foster creativity. At ADE, we incorporate a variety of recreational and leisure opportunities into our program to encourage self-expression, promote positive use of free time, and support a balanced, fulfilling lifestyle.
