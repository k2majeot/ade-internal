Our Mission

At Adult Day Experiences, our mission is to support every individual in developing strong, effective communication skills. We believe that communication is the foundation of independence—and by improving communication, individuals are empowered to recognize and reach their fullest potential.

Our Philosophy

Our program is built around the principles of clarity, connection, and confidence, focusing on communication that is both efficient and meaningful. These skills are essential for navigating daily life, building relationships, and achieving greater autonomy. Patterns=Predictability=Safety= Calm=Connection=Cooperation. It starts with routines: 1. Routines create patterns 2. Patterns create predictability 3. Predictability creates safety 4. Safety creates calm 5. When people feel calm, they are open to connect. 6. When people feel connected, they cooperate. Conscious Discipline

  • 2009-2011

    Our Humble Beginnings

    Adult Day Experiences (ADE) was established in Rocklin, CA in 2010 by Adriene Gaudet, whose background in speech/language pathology (1996-2010) and now over 25 years of experience working with adults with intellectual developmental disabilities (IDD) in day programs and care homes, lead her to create a day program that focuses heavily on an individual’s ability to communicate effectively and advocate for themselves.

  • March 2011

    An Agency is Born

    In March of 2014, ADE, Too! opened, by popular demand. ADE, Too! shares the same program design with the original ADE, but each program is unique based not solely on physical layout, but more importantly, based on the enrolled individuals and how they want their program to be. Having both locations close ensures owner/operator daily oversight, which is a complement to the quality of services our team provides.

  • December 2015

    Transition to Full Service

    A key player in the establishment of our program is our Executive Administrator, Ashli. Ashli and Adriene worked together for 10 years in speech therapy before creating ADE. What started as a conversation on the sidewalk in Auburn, became a dream, and then our reality. Capitalizing on Ashli and Adriene’s knowledge of the population and work in the field, the support of everyone around us, and persistence, ADE has become a rewarding career where we support, advocate, and assist people with IDD.

  • July 2020

    Phase Two Expansion

    ADE places a strong emphasis on promoting an individual’s communication effectiveness. We support all types of communication including verbal, non-verbal, sign language, gestural, no-tech picture communication, alternative or augmentative communication (AAC) including use of dedicated communication tools such as tablets and phones.
